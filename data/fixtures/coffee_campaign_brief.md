# Campaign Brief

## Client
RoastHouse

## Brand
Single-serve coffee pods and ready-to-drink cold brew for everyday coffee
buyers.

## Objective
Identify taste, freshness, and product-positioning risks before scaling creator
and paid social campaigns.

## Target Audience
Keurig users, cold brew drinkers, flavor-sensitive coffee buyers, and shoppers
who read Amazon reviews before trying a new coffee product.

## Product Context
The launch focuses on single-serve pods, flavored coffee, and cold brew
formats. Messaging should be practical about roast level, strength, aroma, and
machine compatibility.

## Required Platforms
TikTok, Instagram Reels, Amazon product detail pages.

## Tone Constraints
Direct, sensory, credible. Avoid inflated premium claims unless review evidence
supports them.

## Claims To Avoid
Do not call a coffee bold, fresh, smooth, Kona, or espresso-like unless the
specific product context and evidence support the claim.

## Campaign Questions
- Which flavor or roast expectations create the most negative reactions?
- Which product claims should creators avoid or clarify?
- Which positive language can become credible creator hooks?
- What should the paid-media team monitor before increasing spend?
